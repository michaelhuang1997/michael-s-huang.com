Hi! I am an incoming PhD student at the Department of Political Science at University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. My current research mainly focuses on China-South Asia relations, political communications, terrorism coverage, and natural language processing. Previously, at the University of Chicago, I was honored to work with Prof. Tyler Williams on the political economy of Ibn-e-Safi's novels and the publishing industries in Pakistan and India, and the paper is available here. In my early projects, I worked on the social and political situations of Tibetan Catholics and conducted ethnography along the Yunnan-Tibet borders under the supervision of Prof. Theodore Pulcini. I graduated with a BA (Hons.) in Political Science and Religion from Dickinson College, Carlisle, PA, whose commitment to liberal arts education has forever urged me to find a balance between humanities and sciences. I also hold a MTS in Religion and Social Science from Harvard University, Cambridge, MA, and a MA in South Asian Languagues from The University of Chicago, Chicago, IL. For more information, please reach out to me at shuyanh2@illinois.edu or michaelhuangshuyan@outlook.com.
